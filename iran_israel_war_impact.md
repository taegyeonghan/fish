# Iran-Israel War: Impact on Global Stock Markets (2026)

## Background
In March 2026, the long-simmering tensions between Iran and Israel escalated into a full-scale military conflict. The United States joined Israel in conducting airstrikes on Iranian nuclear and military facilities. Iran retaliated by effectively closing the Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly 20% of the world's oil is shipped.

## Immediate Market Impact (First Week)
- S&P 500 lost 2% in the first five trading days following the airstrikes
- Morningstar Europe Index fell more than 7%
- MSCI Emerging Markets index declined 8.4%
- S&P 500 had its worst quarterly performance since 2022, dropping nearly 4.6%
- Crude oil prices spiked more than 35%, with Brent crude shooting from ~$70/barrel to as high as $119

## Oil and Energy Crisis
- The closure of the Strait of Hormuz has been characterized by the International Energy Agency as the "largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market"
- Energy-dependent economies in Asia and Europe face severe inflationary pressures
- Oil-exporting nations outside the conflict zone (Saudi Arabia, UAE) face pressure to increase production

## Sector-Specific Impacts
- **Defense/Aerospace**: Companies like Lockheed Martin, Raytheon, Northrop Grumman saw significant stock price increases
- **Energy**: Oil majors (ExxonMobil, Chevron, Shell) benefited from surging crude prices
- **Airlines/Transportation**: Severely impacted by fuel cost increases
- **Technology**: Mixed impact, with some supply chain disruption concerns
- **Financials**: Banks facing uncertainty over loan exposure to affected regions
- **Consumer Staples**: Inflationary pressure affecting margins

## Regional Vulnerability
- Asia appears most vulnerable due to heavy oil import dependence
- Europe faces meaningful exposure through energy costs and proximity
- US dollar strengthened as a safe haven, creating additional pressure on emerging markets
- Global bonds market experienced a significant sell-off

## Key Questions for Investors
1. How long will the Strait of Hormuz remain disrupted?
2. Will diplomatic efforts succeed in brokering a ceasefire?
3. What is the long-term impact on global inflation?
4. How will central banks respond to the simultaneous growth slowdown and inflation spike?
5. Which sectors will emerge stronger post-conflict?

## Historical Context
Historically, equity markets have recovered once geopolitical tensions ease. However, the scale of this conflict and the direct impact on global oil supply chains make this situation potentially more severe than previous Middle East conflicts.
